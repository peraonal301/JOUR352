Consumer Complaints



Aidvantage, a federal loan servicing unit, began servicing loans owned by the U.S. Department of Education in December 2021, according to Student Loan Planner. These loans were previously serviced by Navient, which departed as a loan servicing business for Direct Loans and government-owned Federal Family Education Loan Program Loans. Navient was ranked as one of the least-liked federal student loan servicers in previous Student Loan Planner surveys. While Aidvantage is generally preferred by readers, Student Loan Planner surveys show many of the same complaints as Navient, such as long wait times, inconsistent information and inaccurate monthly payments.

This bar graph depicts the types of complaints and issues from consumers by product. This shows data collected from Jan. 1, 2025, to Jan. 1, 2026. Issues with credit and consumer reports largely outweighed other complaints, reaching over 900,000. During this one-year period, complaints regarding currency, checking and savings, debt and credit were minimal.

This U.S. map is shaded to reflect the number of complaints for each state, with the dark colored states containing larger complaint counts. States in the northern midwest, like Wyoming, Montana, and North and South Dakota, had the least complaints. Larger states along the coasts of the U.S. had the most complaints, with Texas reporting the most at 187,321.
